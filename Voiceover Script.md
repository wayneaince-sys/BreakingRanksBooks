# Voiceover Script — BreakingRanksBooks 30-Second Spot

**Target runtime:** 30.0 seconds
**Voice direction:** Male, mid-to-late register. Plainspoken. Steady, not theatrical. Think a senior NCO who has nothing left to prove. Pace 145–155 wpm. Pauses are intentional — let the silence carry weight.
**Word count:** ~72 words (room for breath)

---

## Scene 1 · 0:00 – 0:05 · Title Open
> *"Some things you carry quiet."*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *"And some things you have to put down."*

**Direction:** Slow, low. Two short statements. Half-second pause between them. Lands as the brass **B** mark and tagline appear.

---

## Scene 2 · 0:05 – 0:10 · Author
> *"Wayne Ince served twenty-three years in the United States Air Force.*
> *Four combat zones. One honest voice."*

**Direction:** Brisker. Matter-of-fact. The credentials speak — don't oversell them.

---

## Scene 3 · 0:10 – 0:15 · The Book
> *"His memoir, Until the Well Runs Dry, is the book he wishes someone had handed him."*

**Direction:** Warmer. Slightly slower on "the book he wishes someone had handed him." This is the emotional pivot of the spot.

---

## Scene 4 · 0:15 – 0:20 · Three Books
> *"Three books — memoir, essays, and a novel.*
> *All written from the inside out."*

**Direction:** Confident, even. Treat the dash as a half-second. "From the inside out" is the credibility line — land it cleanly.

---

## Scene 5 · 0:20 – 0:25 · Buy + Substack
> *"Find them on Amazon.*
> *Read more from Wayne at Wayne A. Ince dot Substack dot com."*

**Direction:** Clear, deliberate. Spell the URL the way you'd dictate it on the phone — separate words, no rush.

---

## Scene 6 · 0:25 – 0:30 · Share + Disclaimer
> *"And if a chapter in here belongs in someone else's hands — send it.*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *These books are not medical advice. If you're in crisis, call or text 988."*

**Direction:** Drop in volume on the disclaimer — calm, not alarmed. The 988 number should land cleanly, almost spoken to one person.

---

## Full continuous read (no scene breaks)

> Some things you carry quiet. And some things you have to put down.
> Wayne Ince served twenty-three years in the United States Air Force. Four combat zones. One honest voice.
> His memoir, *Until the Well Runs Dry*, is the book he wishes someone had handed him.
> Three books — memoir, essays, and a novel. All written from the inside out.
> Find them on Amazon. Read more from Wayne at WayneAInce.Substack.com.
> And if a chapter in here belongs in someone else's hands — send it.
> These books are not medical advice. If you're in crisis, call or text 988.

---

## Recording notes
- **Mic:** Large-diaphragm condenser, close (4–6 inches), pop filter on.
- **Tone:** Aim for intimate — like Wayne is talking to one person, not a crowd.
- **Mix:** Bed of low ambient texture (room tone, not music) under VO. Optional: soft acoustic guitar or a single sustained bass note under the disclaimer.
- **Levels:** VO peaks around –6 dB; bed sits at –22 dB. Disclaimer ducks bed by another 4 dB so the 988 number is the last thing you hear before silence.
- **End on silence:** Final 0.5s should be near-silent black, then logo card holds.

## Alt reads (if needed)
- **Tighter / 25-second version:** drop the second sentence of Scene 4 ("All written from the inside out") and tighten Scene 1 to "Some things you carry quiet." only.
- **Wayne reads it himself:** if Wayne records this in his own voice, no rewrite needed — but ask him to slow Scene 6 by another 10%. He's earned that pause.
